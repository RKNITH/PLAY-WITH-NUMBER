Information About Cardinal Numbers

Cardinal numbers are numbers used for counting or indicating quantity. They represent the size or magnitude of a set, and they are used to answer the question "how many?"

Properties of cardinal numbers:

  • They can be finite (e.g., 1, 2, 3) or infinite (e.g., aleph-null, aleph-one).
  • They are used in arithmetic and set theory to describe the size of sets.
